# Pitch video script — 2 minutes

Answers the five questions the spec asks a pitch to cover: what problem exists,
who has it, what the product does, why Gemini is necessary, and why it could be
a business.

**Format:** talking head or voiceover over screen recording. Screen recording is
safer — it doubles as proof the thing works.

**Total: ~1:55.** Read at a natural pace; don't rush the demo beat.

---

## 0:00–0:20 — The problem (hook with a real number)

> "The average couple spends tens of thousands on a wedding venue — and they
> commit to it after walking through an empty room.
>
> A beige function hall on a Tuesday afternoon looks nothing like a wedding. So
> couples are making the single most expensive decision of the whole event
> almost entirely on imagination. Pinterest just shows you somebody else's
> wedding, in somebody else's venue."

*On screen: an empty, unstyled function room.*

## 0:20–0:35 — Who has it

> "That's every engaged couple — about two million weddings a year in the US
> alone. And it's the venues' problem too: their sales teams have to sell a
> vision of an empty room using a portfolio of other people's weddings."

## 0:35–1:10 — What the product does (live demo)

> "WeddingAI fixes that with the two things every couple already has: a phone,
> and a venue they can stand in.
>
> You walk the room for sixty seconds with the camera running. WeddingAI pulls
> evenly spaced frames straight out of that video, in the browser.
>
> Then Gemini designs a wedding theme from your actual room — not a generic
> mood board. A real palette, decor, florals, and notes about what it noticed in
> your space.
>
> And then it shows you the room wearing it."

*On screen: drop the video → frames extract → theme report with swatches →
"Visualize theme" → the restyled render appears.*

**Land on the render and pause for a full second. This is the moment.**

## 1:10–1:35 — Why Gemini is necessary

> "This can't be built without a multimodal model. The whole point is that the
> design comes from *your* room — Gemini has to look at the real space, reason
> about its light and architecture, return a structured design an interface can
> actually render, and then generate a photorealistic restyle anchored to that
> same room.
>
> A text model could write you a mood board. Only Gemini can look at your venue
> and hand it back transformed."

## 1:35–1:55 — Why it's a business

> "Venues have a direct commercial incentive here: a couple who can *see* the
> room styled is far likelier to book it. That's a B2B motion with a short sales
> cycle, where every venue brings its own couples.
>
> And once Gemini has produced a specific palette and decor list, every item in
> it is something you can buy — florists, rentals, stylists.
>
> WeddingAI is how you walk into your wedding before it exists."

---

## Delivery notes

- **Do not oversell the 3D.** Say "builds toward a 3D scene you can walk
  through" — the deployed app shows a placeholder. If a judge opens the viewer
  and sees a placeholder after you claimed a walkthrough, you lose more
  credibility than the claim ever bought.
- The strongest 5 seconds is the restyled render appearing. Cut everything else
  before cutting that.
- Say "Gemini" explicitly. Don't say "AI" when you mean Gemini.
