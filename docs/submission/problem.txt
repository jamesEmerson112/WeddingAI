PROBLEM STATEMENT
=================
Couples commit tens of thousands to a wedding venue after touring an empty room.

The gap between "beige function hall on a Tuesday afternoon" and "our wedding"
is enormous, and nothing currently bridges it:

- Venue tours show the space unstyled, empty, and under fluorescent light.
- Mood boards and Pinterest show other people's weddings in other people's
  venues, which tells you nothing about how YOUR room will look.
- Professional visualisation and 3D renders exist, but cost more than most
  couples' entire decor budget and take weeks to turn around.

The result is that the single most expensive decision of the whole event is
made almost entirely on imagination. The uncertainty doesn't resolve at
booking either - the "will this actually look like anything?" anxiety runs all
the way to the day itself, and drives both overspending on decor and regret
afterwards.


TARGET USER
===========
Primary: engaged couples who are choosing between venues, or who have booked
one and are deciding how to style it. They are non-technical, working to a
fixed budget, and typically planning around full-time jobs.

Secondary: wedding planners and venue sales teams, who have the same problem
from the other side - they must sell a vision of an empty room to a sceptical
couple, and currently do it with a portfolio of somebody else's wedding.


WHY IT HASN'T BEEN SOLVED
=========================
Photorealistic restyling of a specific real room used to require either a 3D
artist or a photogrammetry pipeline plus manual set dressing. Both are too slow
and too expensive for a consumer purchase. What changed is that a multimodal
model can now look at real photos of a specific space, reason about its light
and architecture, and generate a coherent restyle anchored to that room.
