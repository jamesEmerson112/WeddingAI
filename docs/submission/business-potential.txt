BUSINESS POTENTIAL
==================

The market
----------
The US wedding industry is roughly $70bn a year, and the venue is the single
largest line item in a typical budget. Around 2 million weddings happen in the
US annually, and essentially every one of them involves a couple trying to
picture an empty room dressed for their day.

Why this is a business and not a feature
----------------------------------------
The pain is acute, time-boxed and attached to a decision people are already
spending heavily on. Couples routinely pay for photographers, planners and
mood-boarding services; visualisation sits naturally in that same budget line
and is currently either unavailable or priced for commercial clients.

Three routes to revenue
-----------------------
1. Consumer subscription. A couple pays a small one-off or monthly fee during
   the planning window (typically 6-14 months) to visualise venues and themes.
   Naturally time-limited, but high intent and low price sensitivity relative
   to the overall budget.

2. Venue and planner B2B - the stronger motion. Venues have a direct commercial
   incentive: a couple who can see the room styled is far likelier to book.
   Selling per-venue seats to venues and planning firms gives recurring revenue,
   a much shorter sales cycle than consumer acquisition, and each venue brings
   its own couples. The 3D walkthrough becomes a sales asset the venue owns.

3. Decor and rental marketplace. Once Gemini has produced a specific palette,
   decor list and floral plan, every item in it is a purchasable object.
   Affiliate or marketplace revenue on florists, rentals and stylists converts
   the visualisation into a transaction, and is the highest-ceiling path.

Defensibility
-------------
The capture pipeline is the moat, not the prompt. Getting a usable 3D
reconstruction from a non-technical user is the hard part, and the video-to-
frames approach removes the step that kills adoption - nobody will shoot 40-150
overlapping photos by hand, but anyone can walk a room with the camera running.
Accumulated venue reconstructions also compound: once a venue is captured, every
future couple considering it can be sold instantly.

Adjacent markets
----------------
The same capture-design-render loop applies with little change to event spaces,
corporate functions, interior design, real estate staging and short-term rental
listings. Weddings are the wedge because the emotional stakes and the budget are
both unusually high.
